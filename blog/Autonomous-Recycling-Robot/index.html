Autonomous Recycling Robot

The infamous UBC Engineering Physics robot summer... but remote!

Every year, UBC's Engineering Physics program runs a project course that is commonly known as "robot summer". The course challenges us to build an autonomous robot from scratch (including the electronics, the firmware, the mechanical parts) to complete a challenge assigned to us. Generally, students get to work with cool laser and waterjet cutters and build complex robots that can drive, zipline, retrieve objects using their acrylic/hardwood/sheet metal robots that like this: However, my class unfortunately became the guinea pigs for the first remote version of the course (thanks COVID), so our robots ended up looking more like this:

That being said, I still learned a lot from the overall experience. I learned how easy it is to build a moving robot from off-the-shelf items like plastic boards. I learned that glue gun glue is actually very heavy. I learned how to solder for the first time, got to work with various analog circuits, and in the process learn about embedded systems through a hands-on project. I could go on for different "things I learned" from the project, and I guess the most important thing I learned from this is how to tackle a multidisciplinary project and integrate different engineering specialties (ie. mechanical, firmware, software, electrical) together. For my part, I dived right into >our circuitry and our Arduino-based codebase.
